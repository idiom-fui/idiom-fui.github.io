Toward Next Generation I/O Performance Analysis

Supported by the French BPI / Region Ile de France

Integrated Device I/O Monitor

Concept Consortium

IDIOM is a French-funded research project aiming at observing and analyzing the I/O activity across the whole software stack: from the application down to the kernel managed sotrage device.

The scope of applicability encompasses HPC and Cloud based applications. IDIOM software is open-source and non-intrusive, leveraging shared library overloading I/O calls are intercepted and trace with a lightweight mechanism. The results are analysis using novel congestion detection algorithms to spot quickly any issues hurting applications performance.

5 Participants

IDIOM consortium includes leading organizations both from the academic and industry. Participants are bringing their expertise and use-cases to deliver industrial grade solutions to real world problems.

4 pillars

The contribution of IDIOM is organized on 4 pillars: Tracing, Transparency, Analysis and Exploitation .

Achievements

IDIOM has delivered several contribution to the community:

  • Open-source I/O tracer: EZTrace has been extended to trace communications and I/O activities
  • Kernel monitoring: opening-up the kernel black-box and trace an I/O request from application to kernel space
  • Congestion Analysis: leveraging traces to understand and quantify the impact of I/O bottlenecks
  • Schedulig Policies: making the job scheduler aware of I/O and interference for a better efficiency
